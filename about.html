I'm Gabriele Saraceno

Computers have always been in my life. I woke up on the Christmas of 2008 with my first PC game, The Orange Box, a compilation of video games developed by Valve Software. When my older brother introduced me into the world of computers, I fell in love immediately. I went from playing video games to dreaming of programming them and eventually in my junior year of high school. That's where the start of all my passions went full circle.

On the flip side of everything, I've been skateboarding since I was five. I have grown up riding skateboards because it's a rush that you can't really top, the satisfaction of landing something as simple as an Ollie on a curb.

Graphic design and photography came from my appreciation of art. In the 8th grade, I got my first little point-and-shoot camera and took that everywhere I went, making little films with my cousins and taking photos of the Puerto Rican landscape. My freshman year of high school, I took Intro to Photography and learned how to edit my own photos in Photoshop, to developing film, creating a love for 35mm photography. Once I learned one skill, I felt the need to learn how to design too. I had terrible drawing skills, but I thought I was creative enough to learn Illustrator. This is how I became who I am.
